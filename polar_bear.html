Polar Bears

Habitat

Polar Bears almost exclusively inhabit the Artic Circle and roam around on the ice caps.
Due to their very unpredicatable enviroment polar bears, unlike other bears, don't make dens.
Instead they roam areas that can span into hundreds of Kiometers

Diet

Contrary to most bears that are omnivorous, the polar bear is carnivourous.
However, this is due to the harsh enviroment, with limited prey and a harsh energy consuming enviromet
polar bears opt for the most efficient animals to hunt, seals. They have also occasional gone for
other artic sea life such as; walrsues, narwhals, and dead whale remains.

Unique Characteristics

Polar bears have a number of unique behaviors that differentiate them from other species:

  • Polar bears have developed a method for seal hunting, they wait near holes in the ice for seals to surface.

  • Due to the extreme cold of the Artic Circle, polar bears have to consume atleast 2kg of Fat

  • Polar bears spend a majority of their time at the icy Artic sea hunting for food, this has led to them being classified as a marine mammal.

  • Polar bears have adapted to their enviroment and can swim for days at a time, hunting prey or relocating
