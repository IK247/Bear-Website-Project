Bears


What Are Bears?

Bears are an omnivorous animal that can be found in the Americas, Asia and Europe.
They stand one to three meters tall but walk on all fours. They resemble a larger dog, with stout legs
a snout and stubby ears.There are 8 species of Bears; Sloth Bear, Black Bears, Sun Bears, Asiatic Black Bear,
Spectacled Bear, with the Panda Bear, Brown(or Grizzly) Bear,the Polar Bear being the most recognizable ones.

General Facts

  • Bears can spend up to 100 hours in hibernation

  • Bears live 25 years in the wild but can live up to 50 in captivity

  • All Bears make their homes, or den by digging into the ground. Some however use natural caves.

  • Bears can acheive top speeds of 56kmh

  • Bears are primarily solitary creatures, except for a mother nursing her cubs

  • Six out of the 8 bear species are currently endangered species

  • Bears have two layers of fur, one for insulation and the other for water-proofing

  • Unlike most mammals, Bears have eyesight that can almost rivaal a humans. They also see in color
